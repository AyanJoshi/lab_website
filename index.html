About

The Autonomous Intelligent Mobile Systems Laboratory (AIMS Lab) was established in 2021 as one of the major labs at the Louisville Automation and Robotics Research Institute (LARRI) at the University of Louisville.


AIMS Lab conducts advanced studies and development of autonomous and intelligent systems including unmanned aerial vehicles, ground rovers, connected and autonomous vehicles, industrial robotic arms, metaverse applications, and wireless/mobile innovations. Our research integrates novel sensing and intelligent decision-making in collaborative robotics using AI and adaptive sensing, along with multi-level edge and cloud computing.


We provide an open, inclusive, and collaborative environment, embracing researchers from diverse backgrounds. Our cutting-edge facility is equipped with mobile robots, sensors, systems, and tools, and connects with several other labs at LARRI for interdisciplinary collaboration.

Lab Facilities & Equipment

AIMS Lab inhabits a large high-bay space at LARRI, optimized for indoor aerial robotic flight and ground vehicle control. High-precision tracking is facilitated by an optical tracking system using 22 OptiTrack cameras that provides over 12,000 cubic feet of test room with millimeter-precision reporting.

Indoor fully netted drone flying facility with OptiTrack motion capture system and multiple advanced drones

Ground robots and autonomous electric vehicle with available infrastructure for indoor experiments

Franka-Emika robotic arm with Haption haptic device, AR/VR sensors, RGBD cameras, and Ouster LiDARs

Computing facility with embedded computing units, Lambda vector server, and Software-Defined Radios for 6G research
